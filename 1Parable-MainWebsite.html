Jesus taught through parables, which are short stories designed to teach a spiritual lesson. Mark's Gospel says, "He did not say anything to them without using a parable" (Mark 4:34).

Jesus had the wisdom to simplify the significant spiritual truths he needed to share with humanity into relevant, understandable parables.
A parable is a story that uses a basic, everyday subject to teach a deeper, more meaningful moral lesson.
The source meaning of "parable" is "the placement of two things side by side for the purpose of comparison."
